I used to play tons of Massively Multiplayer Online (MMO) games. Everquest, Ultima Online, Dark Age of Camelot, Shadowbane, Auto Assault, Everquest 2, Guild Wars (not technically an MMO, I don't care), Tabula Rasa, City of Heroes, Lineage 2... the list does actually keep going on. Lots of free Korean ones over the years too. Over the years I've fallen off - I ran a hardcore raiding guild for years and don't have it in me to take on that full time job again. I don't even have the time to play casually unless I solo and I really like playing WITH people. But...

I've been playing two recently. City of Heroes and Everquest. Lets start with the more disappointing one first:

## Everquest

This is my first MMO love. I started raiding in this, multiboxing (playing multiple characters at once), I played live for 10 years, and I've played a handful of emulated servers over the years including retro style (Project 1999), attempts to emulate the same experience as live, and hyper custom (Scorpius2k and Shards of Dalaya) variants.

Nothing was ever like the live experience for me because it didn't include my friends. That's impossible to recreate. So I never stuck with any of them but then... Everquest Legends gets announced. A server that emphasises solo and small group play? An MMO that doesn't waste your time?! Grindaverse, gear agnostic (mostly), with a fresh (to me) spin on EQ? Hell yeah! So the gist for those unaware is that it turns your single character per server into every possible character. Class, Race, and Deity selections are changeable on the fly and instead of having a single class you can have up to 3. Cool beans. They've made more changes but those are out of the scope of what I want to talk about here because... I kind of hate it. Sort of.

Everquest was launched in 1999, there have been emulated servers for over 20 years, This isn't a beta product. This isn't new territory the game is covering (there are at least a couple private servers that do this same triple classing thing). And yet during this beta, that as of my writing this is meant to launch in 15 days, they've kept most changes in the game to large SQL query based rewrites. I word it like that because it's easier to overwrite a zones spawn tables of mobs with the same table, and just collide it with a list of dual class combinations so that instead of have just snakes you get wizard/cleric snakes or instead of normal skeletons they can be paladin/mage skelies. This is a very low effort change. This isn't a thoughtful change where they're changing things like itemization, adding new camps (static spawned areas), questlines, stories etc to the game. It's sloppy. In doing this, and related rewrites of vendor tables, they've removed basic adventuring goods (spell reagents) from cities that those classes can't start at, they've broken static spawns all over the world. They've added ~~3~~ 2 (with a third promised by launch) races into the game that weren't in launch EQ, but they have what are effectively placeholder starting zones with no quests (the game, remember, is called everQUEST).

So have they done anything other than that? Yeah - most of their work has clearly gone into the triple class system, the ability to spin up instances of any zone, and the ability to level up items by merging it with additional copies of itself. They've also gone ahead and "revamped" 3 zones with new loot. 3 zones out of the 100 or so in the game currently. Revamped meaning they've changed the loot of the already existing spawns a bit. Minor changes. Boring changes. The breaking of things combined with the lack of meaningful new content is disappointing. The instancing system is buggy, login issues abound, the end game is nonexistent. This feels like a threadbare tech demo more than a viable product that's launching in 15 days. I'll keep checking in and make another post when my sub is up in late August.

Oh that too: it has a box price (20), a monthly sub (10), and a cash shop (to unlock storage, which you might need a lot of in a game where you're limited to a single character who can be countless sub characters.) That's a bad vibe. The community is pretty hostile to people bringing this up (get a real job you scrub) but it doesn't sit well with me. Also the community is full of obnoxious twits - more so than most MMOs. A mix of entitlement, dev-Staning, pick mes, and people just looking to sow discontent.



## City of Heroes

I'm playing on the Homecoming server, Excelsior to be specific. I have a level 50 fire/fire tank that I didn't make explicitly to be a farming character, I just made it because it's fun to be a big ole splashy bomb of a character. I have numerous alts. I mostly like it. It's fun - it's also very low stakes. Leveling up is much faster than it was on live, building interesting characters is fairly easy, and there are plenty of teams going during the evenings when I can play. I do think their team is a bit careless in some of the changes they make to specific powersets (a character is an archetype which is the kind of class they are, and then a combination of a primary and secondary powerset. These can be things like using assault rifles, having elemental defenses, the kind of pets they summon... it's pretty varied) and the community seems to have a bit of ADHD - teams don't tend to last very long so you're not really playing with the same people very often and making friends.

But still - it's been a lot of fun. And some of the story lines are super dope still. Most are dumb comic bullshit, but that's what the game is meant to be.



So those are my thoughts on the two MMOs I've been playing lately - EQLegends is disappointing and CoH is surprisingly appointing.
