So there is a new Everquest server coming out. I'd say a new ruleset but I think that is burying the lead a bit. Everquest legends - a grandiose title for ... one of the servers of all time. So here's the deal: lot of changes on this server.

- Triple classing - select 3 character classes instead of one
- Class changing - you can change classes not quite on the fly but close to it
- Race/Deity changing - this is more flavor than anything but hey it is different
- a focus on Solo and "Casual" play. That's per the devs, the quotes are there for reasons we'll get into later.
- Classic EQ only so far - the original 120 or so zones.
- "Revitalizing" zones with new loot. Quotes again for a reason.
- A item/spell upgrading system that encourages grinding farming. Very casual, much wow.
- An ad hoc instancing system: Instance any zone including dungeons, open world areas, raids, and cities. This is neat.
- A difficulty system, allowing 5 selectable difficulty ratings that unlock multi classing for enemy mobs, more difficult encounters (read that largely as HP bloat and hilariously increase aggro ranges), and better loot (items drop in their upgraded states).

That's a lot, and it would be a lot for any dev team but this dev team is either lazy, incompetent, or maybe just overly confident and I'm annoyed that I can't tell which it is. The game launches in 5 days and the "pre order beta" is over, servers are shut down and being wiped. A short list of things that stuck out to me as being less than fully baked:

Classes: There are 16 classes in EverQuest and many of them are sorely lacking in cool, special stuff, compared to the others. Berzerkers are only useful for a stance (more on stances later) that is unique to them, they bring nothing else unique or cool to the table. Warriors are strictly better than them in every other way. Paladins and Shadowknights too. Speaking of: Shadowknights - their Harm Touch ability is on a 20 minute cooldown and does a moderate amount of damage to an enemy. Paladin's Lay on Hands which is meant to be a mirrored ability does a full and complete heal of a character at any level and recharges in 15 minutes. One of those is strictly speaking better than the other. Mages and Necros: Pets spent the entire beta somewhat broken (half hitpoints if you had an intelligence caster as your primary class, wtf) and their stats are still not fully implemented. Rogue's backstab is amazing till level 20 then falls off of a cliff and at higher levels their poisons are nearly worthless due to mob resists. Beastlords... I don't have enough room or time for this but they're in a bad way. Their pets are awful, their main kind of weapon basically isn't in the game, their melee skills pale in comparison to Monks which are their closest counterpart. Cleric's unique, best ability the res is meaningless in a game with in zone respawns and no death penalty and nothing was given to them to replace it. I want to stress, I'm being brief here.

Instances and Difficulty settings: Difficulty settings primarily are more difficult by giving mobs more abilities and hitpoints as well as increasing aggro ranges. Aggro can go through walls, doors, floors, etc now in a way that was rare on live which makes Paladins or Bards almost necessary for their calming abilities or a group that can kill 16+ things at once - this isn't very "casual" friendly. Instances are kind of a good idea, but a lot of changes that were made to this game hack away at the social timbers that supported it and this is a big one. The world is empty because you're always off in your own isolated corners of it.

Zone revamps: These aren't really zone revamps so much as reitemization passes, they added new items which are pretty powerful to a handful of dungeons. That's kind of cool, but A) it's weird to get an item that you carry from 10 through to 45, B) there are 19 dungeons in basic EQ and they remade 5 of them during the beta. Launching with less than all 19 just gives the impression that this is launching as a beta product. Which they then reinforce on their discord by constantly saying "this is a live service game that will change and grow after launch".

Race unlocks: Not much to say, these are just faction grinds of vary lengths. Some can be done with a handful of money in a few minutes, Ogres take 400+ quest turns ins + 20 hours of grinding foraged berries. If that sounds underwhelming and boring that is because it is.

Group XP: Group XP is an even split. This is entirely fair, if you kill a mob with 4 people you get 1/4 of the experience. The problem is that if you have 4 people, even if you increase your kill speed by 4 times (unlikely, someone has to be a support character, right?) you don't get 4 times as many mobs and even one or two people can keep most reasonable areas of mobs cleared before respawn timers. Groups then add down time which is boring, especially in a game that otherwise goes to minimize downtime.

Stance dancing: All classes have stances and managing what stance you're in is a super important part of "high level" play... but it's deeply uninteresting. Get hit alot? Evasive stance (better have monk, rogue, beastlord, or berzerker in your class loadout strictly for that one stance because 3 of those are worthless outside of it). Need to do more damage? Offensive stance doubles your melee damage. Getting interrupted? Invocation stance and you have quick casting spells, but long global cooldowns. It's not interesting, it's just a management game.

I've got more quibbles, but the point I'm driving towards here is that this game launches in 5 days and is in the same state I would have it in to pitch the idea. This is a product made by former private server devs, under the aegis of the company that owns the EQ IP. In that context this is trash. Even the whole idea of it is trash, stick with me for a moment.

Everquest is at best a rudimentary game. It was referred to as a chatroom with swords back in it's heyday which demonstrates two super important things about the game. The first is that the social elements of the game are extremely important - the game truly is the friends we made along the way. The second is evident after playing for a bit - it's all auto attack and spell cast cooldowns. There's not a whole lot of activity in the game, the stances I complained about above are attempting to add something to that but they just don't do a good job of doing it. What they've done is create an online only game, with a monthly fee, that you're encouraged to solo in, that was only good because of the social features. That sucks.

The fixes to this are simple: Give people a reason to go into non instanced zones so that it is more social. Make open world zones have much lower spawn timers so that people aren't just looping the same few dungeons. *ADD QUESTS TO A GAME WITH QUEST IN ITS DAMN NAME*. Stop discouraging grouping up with slower (though fair, I'll admit) XP rates. Spend some time actually designing and developing your game which is an utterly wild concept. I've annoyed myself by writing this all out. 

I would also encourage policing the community a bit more - the global in game chats are toxic and I've never had to block as many people as I have on the discord in the past few days. Like all online games are a little bit toxic but this is worse than League ever was. Devs have stated there will be no active moderation of in game chat and that players should get used to using /ignore commands liberally. Bold position to take in 2026.

That's it, that's my rant. I'll have something more positive next time. Maybe. It is me after all, I'm always a bit goaty.
