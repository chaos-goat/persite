I'm writing this while sitting in the ending minutes of a panel at our local comic con, it's the audience Q and A portion and is mostly people "asking questions" to hear themselves speak. This is normal and not what I think marks the decline of the panel as both an act of community building and one of socialization.

I have some opinions about these fandom events and the over commercialization of everything. As someone that has no interest in selfies or autographs with celebrities, niche or otherwise, and someone who is pretty picky about what they'll pick up in a vendor hall (after too many years of not being picky) the main draw to these fan events are the community aspects. I want to meet people and make friends which is something I've found quite hard to do as an adult. Making acquaintances and being friendly is easy but transitioning to friends has generally proven trickier.

So how does this intertwine with panels? That was one of the main ways that people socialized! And still might be, maybe I've just gotten old. A lot of the media related panels seem to take the form of a bad top 10 list or "aw man that's such a good movie!" and aren't substantive in any sort of interesting way. Like I don't give a shit that you found x interesting, I care WHY you found it interesting. And I want to know especially if it's something I didn't like. But when panels are just half assed nostalgia fests it really ruins it for me,
